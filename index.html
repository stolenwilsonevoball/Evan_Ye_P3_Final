Interactive Data Story

Where the Money Is in Esports

Esports includes many different games, but the money is not spread evenly. This project uses player earnings and tournament prize data to show how a relatively small number of games, events, and countries shape most of the financial landscape.

Section 1

Which games dominate player earnings?

The first view looks at total player winnings by game. This sets up the larger story by showing that the esports space is broad, but not financially balanced.

Section 2

Country rankings tell a similar story

The second view shifts from games to countries. Instead of asking which titles are largest, it asks where the highest-earning players are concentrated.

Section 3

Explore one game at a time

This is the main interaction in the project. Selecting a game updates the section below so users can compare how individual titles differ in terms of country representation, player counts, and tournament presence.

Section 4

Looking at tournaments for the selected game

The tournament view focuses on prize money and scale. Hovering over a point reveals the tournament, while the selected game keeps the comparison specific instead of overwhelming.

Section 5

The overall pattern is concentration

The final section returns to the full dataset and summarizes the main pattern. Even with many games represented, a small group of titles takes up a large share of the money.
